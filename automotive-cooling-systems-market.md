<p><p>The automotive cooling systems market consists of two main types: liquid-cooled and air-cooled. Liquid-cooled systems utilize a mixture of water and coolant to absorb heat generated by the engine and transfer it away through a radiator. This type of system provides more efficient cooling, enabling engines to operate at optimal temperatures. On the other hand, air-cooled systems rely on air circulation to cool the engine, often through fins or a fan. While simpler and cheaper, air-cooled systems are less efficient and may lead to higher engine temperatures in demanding conditions.</p></p>
