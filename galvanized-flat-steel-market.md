<p><p>The galvanized flat steel market comprises two types: hot-dip galvanized flat steel and cold galvanized flat steel. Hot-dip galvanized flat steel is created by immersing the steel in a bath of molten zinc, which forms a protective layer on its surface. This type of galvanized steel is highly durable and finds applications in construction, automotive, and manufacturing industries. On the other hand, cold galvanized flat steel involves a process of electroplating zinc onto the steel surface. Although not as long-lasting as hot-dip galvanized steel, cold galvanized steel is mostly used in less demanding applications.</p></p>
