<p><p>A commercial vehicle brake caliper is a component of the braking system that houses the brake pads and applies pressure to the brake rotor, causing the vehicle to slow down or stop. It is a crucial part of the braking mechanism in commercial vehicles such as trucks, buses, and delivery vans. The caliper is responsible for converting hydraulic pressure from the brake pedal into mechanical force, which clamps the brake pads onto the rotor, thereby generating friction and halting the vehicle.</p><p>The market analysis of the commercial vehicle brake caliper sector indicates a steady growth trajectory. <p><strong>The Commercial Vehicle Brake Caliper Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Single Piston Caliper</li><li>Multi-Piston Caliper</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The commercial vehicle brake caliper market consists of two main types: single piston caliper and multi-piston caliper. Single piston calipers have a single piston that applies pressure on the brake pads to create friction and slow down the vehicle. They are typically simpler and less expensive. In contrast, multi-piston calipers have multiple pistons that distribute the force more evenly, resulting in improved braking performance and heat dissipation. These are often found in high-performance vehicles or heavy-duty applications where increased braking power is required.</p></p>
