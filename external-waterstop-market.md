<p><p>An external waterstop is a construction material used to prevent the entry of water or any other liquid substance into a structure at its joints or seams. It is typically employed in the construction of water-retaining structures such as dams, reservoirs, swimming pools, tunnels, and basements. The primary function of an external waterstop is to create a watertight barrier at the joint or seam, ensuring the structural integrity of the building or infrastructure.</p><p>The external waterstop market is experiencing steady growth and is expected to continue expanding in the coming years. The increasing demand for water-retaining structures and the growing awareness regarding the importance of waterproofing in construction projects are the major driving factors for this market.</p><p>With the rising population and urbanization, the construction industry is witnessing rapid growth worldwide.
