<p><p>Composite materials are widely used in the automotive industry due to their lightweight and high strength properties. Three common types of composites used in this market are Sheet Molding Compound (SMC), Fiber Reinforced Polymer (FRP), and Resin Transfer Molding (RTM). SMC is made of polyester resin combined with glass or carbon fibers and is commonly used for exterior body parts. FRP consists of a polymer matrix reinforced with fibers such as carbon or glass and is used for various car components. RTM involves injecting liquid resin into a mold with pre-placed fibers, resulting in strong and complex shapes for automotive applications.</p></p>
