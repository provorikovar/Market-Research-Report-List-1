<p><p>Mineral waxes are a type of waxes derived from minerals or fossil fuels. Peat waxes are obtained from partially decomposed plant material found in marshy areas. Ozocerite is a naturally occurring wax obtained from certain rocks, predominantly in Eastern Europe. Montan wax is derived from lignite coal and is known for its high melting point and excellent emulsifying properties. Ceresin waxes are produced from ozokerite or petroleum, and are primarily used in the cosmetics and pharmaceutical industries for their moisturizing and binding properties.</p></p>
