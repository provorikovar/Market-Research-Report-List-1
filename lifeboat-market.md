The market growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing number of offshore installations, such as oil rigs and wind farms, has led to a higher demand for lifeboats as a safety measure. These offshore structures require efficient means of evacuation in case of emergencies, driving the market growth.</p><p>Additionally, stringent safety regulations imposed by governing bodies, such as the International Maritime Organization (IMO), have further boosted the demand for lifeboats. Ships and offshore structures are required to meet specific safety standards, including the provision of sufficient lifeboats for passengers and crew. <p><p>The lifeboat market consists of three main types: partially enclosed lifeboats, totally enclosed lifeboats, and open lifeboats. Partially enclosed lifeboats have a roof structure but are open on the sides, providing protection from rain and wind. Totally enclosed lifeboats have a fully enclosed structure with windows and airtight doors, ensuring greater safety in extreme weather conditions or rough seas. Open lifeboats, on the other hand, are simple and open vessels without any protection, commonly used in calmer waters or short distances. Manufacturers cater to the varied needs and preferences of customers within this market.</p></p>
