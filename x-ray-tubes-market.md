<p><p>X-ray tubes are devices used in medical imaging to generate X-rays for diagnostic purposes. They are also used in various industrial applications including non-destructive testing and material analysis. X-ray tubes consist of a cathode, an anode, and a vacuum tube that is surrounded by a metal casing. When high voltage is applied to the tube, electrons are accelerated towards the anode, causing the release of X-rays.</p><p>The X-ray tubes market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, growing geriatric population, and the rising demand for minimally invasive procedures are driving the market growth. Additionally, advancements in X-ray technology, such as the development of digital detectors, are further contributing to market expansion. The global market is also benefiting from the rising adoption of X-ray tubes in emerging economies and the increasing use of X-ray imaging in various industries beyond healthcare.</p><p>Moreover, the market is experiencing a shift towards the development of portable and lightweight X-ray machines, which offer greater flexibility and convenience in terms of usage. This trend is mainly driven by the demand for point-of-care diagnostics and the need for X-ray imaging in remote or resource-limited areas.</p><p>However, the X-ray tubes market faces challenges associated with the high cost of X-ray machines, the shortage of skilled professionals, and concerns regarding radiation exposure.
