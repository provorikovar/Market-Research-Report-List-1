<p><p>Perfluorinated type plastic optical fiber (POF) is a flexible and transparent fiber used for transmitting data through light signals. It is primarily classified into three market types: extrusion type, diffusion type, and other markets. </p><p>Extrusion type POF is produced by extruding a perfluorinated polymer material into a fiber shape. It is known for its high transmission capacity and low optical loss, making it suitable for high-speed communication applications. </p><p>Diffusion type POF is manufactured by diffusing a perfluorinated polymer onto a glass or plastic core. This type provides improved stability and lower transmission loss compared to extrusion type POF. </p><p>Other markets in the POF industry include specialty applications like sensing, lightning, automotive, and medical sectors, where perfluorinated type POF is utilized for various specific purposes.</p></p>
