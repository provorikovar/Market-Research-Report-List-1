<p><p>Microcatheters are small, thin catheters used in medical procedures to access and deliver treatments or diagnose conditions within the body. The Delivery market type refers to microcatheters used to deliver substances such as medication, chemotherapy drugs, or embolic materials to specific sites within the body. Diagnosis microcatheters are used to guide the insertion of other diagnostic tools, like imaging agents or sensors, to accurately diagnose medical conditions. Aspiration microcatheters are used to remove fluids, tissues, or blockages from the body. Finally, Steerable microcatheters are designed to navigate through complex and tortuous anatomical structures for precise treatment delivery.</p></p>
