<p><p>Oriented Strand Board (OSB) is a type of engineered wood made from small wooden strands that are oriented in different directions and then compressed and bonded together with resin. There are four different market types for OSB: OSB/1, OSB/2, OSB/3, and OSB/4. OSB/1 is mainly used for non-structural applications, such as packaging and furniture backing. OSB/2 is suitable for load-bearing applications in dry conditions, such as wall sheathing. OSB/3 is designed for load-bearing applications in humid conditions, such as flooring and walls in kitchens or bathrooms. Lastly, OSB/4 is ideal for heavy-duty load-bearing applications, including flooring in high-traffic areas and industrial manufacturing.</p></p>
