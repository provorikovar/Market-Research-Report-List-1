<p><p>The pure vanilla extract market consists of two main types: liquid and powder. Liquid vanilla extract is the most common form and is made by extracting vanilla beans in an alcohol-water solution. It has a strong, concentrated flavor and is ideal for recipes requiring liquid ingredients. On the other hand, vanilla powder is made by drying and grinding vanilla beans into a fine powder. It is less concentrated but provides a convenient option for recipes where a liquid form may not be suitable. Both liquid and powder vanilla extract offer consumers versatile options for adding authentic vanilla flavor to their culinary creations.</p></p>
