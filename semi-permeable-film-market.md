<p><p>Semi-permeable film refers to a type of material that allows certain substances to pass through while restricting the passage of others. Biomembranes are natural semi-permeable films found in living organisms, such as cell membranes. They control the movement of molecules and ions in and out of cells. Synthetic membranes, on the other hand, are human-made semi-permeable films designed for various applications, including water filtration, gas separation, and drug delivery. These membranes are often tailored to have specific permeability properties, making them valuable in a wide range of industrial and biomedical sectors.</p></p>
