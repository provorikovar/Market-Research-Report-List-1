Refrigerated and frozen storage involve maintaining samples at controlled temperatures to preserve their integrity. Ultra-low storage uses extremely low temperatures, such as liquid nitrogen, for long-term sample preservation. The "Others" category comprises specialized storage options, like cryogenic storage for stem cells or tissue samples. These storage types cater to different biostorage needs, depending on the sample's perishability and preservation requirements.</p></p>
