<p><p>Light Fidelity (Li-Fi) is a wireless communication technology that utilizes visible light to transmit data. The Li-Fi market comprises three main types: LED, photodetectors, and microcontrollers (MCU). LEDs are used as the light source, emitting modulated light signals for data transmission. Photodetectors capture and convert the light signals into electrical signals, enabling data reception. Microcontrollers act as the control units, managing the modulation and demodulation processes. These three components collectively form the Li-Fi ecosystem, playing a crucial role in establishing high-speed, secure, and energy-efficient communication networks through visible light.</p></p>
