<p><p>The highly stable heat transfer fluids market can be divided into two types: organic compound and mineral compound. Organic compound heat transfer fluids are synthetic fluids that are typically made from hydrocarbon-based materials. They offer excellent resistance to oxidation and thermal degradation, making them suitable for high-temperature applications. On the other hand, mineral compound heat transfer fluids are based on inorganic salts and other minerals. They provide good thermal stability and low viscosity, making them ideal for low-temperature operations. Both types of compounds contribute to the efficient transfer of heat in various industrial processes.</p></p>
