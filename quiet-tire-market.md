<p><p>A quiet tire, also known as a low-noise tire or a noise-reducing tire, is a type of tire designed to minimize noise and vibrations while driving. Firstly, the rising awareness among consumers about noise pollution and its impact on health and well-being has driven the demand for quieter vehicles, including tires. Additionally, the growing popularity of electric and hybrid vehicles, which are generally quieter than vehicles with internal combustion engines, has further fueled the demand for quiet tires.</p><p>Furthermore, stringent noise emission regulations imposed by various governments across the globe have played a significant role in promoting the adoption of quiet tires. These regulations require vehicle manufacturers to equip their vehicles with tires that meet specific noise emission standards, thus creating a favorable market for quiet tire manufacturers.</p><p>In terms of the latest trends, tire manufacturers are continuously investing in research and development to improve tire technology and reduce noise levels further. <p><p>The tire market is categorized into two types: radial tire market and bias tire market. Radial tires are constructed with layers of cords that run across the tire from bead to bead, ensuring smooth and comfortable rides with improved grip and fuel efficiency. Bias tires, on the other hand, have overlapping plies that crisscross each other diagonally, providing better load-carrying capacity and durability but with less traction and higher rolling resistance. These tire types cater to different vehicles and usage requirements, offering distinct performance characteristics and price ranges.</p></p>
