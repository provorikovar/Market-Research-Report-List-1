<p><p>Thermally-Modified Timber (TMT) refers to a type of wood that has undergone a process of thermal modification to enhance its properties. There are two main market types for TMT: Thermo-S TMT and Thermo-D TMT. Thermo-S TMT involves heating the wood to a moderate temperature, resulting in improved durability, stability, and resistance to decay. Thermo-D TMT, on the other hand, involves subjecting the wood to higher temperatures, leading to increased strength, reduced moisture absorption, and improved dimensional stability. Both market types offer unique benefits and are gaining popularity in various industries.</p></p>
