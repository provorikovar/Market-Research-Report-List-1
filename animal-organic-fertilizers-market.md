<p><p>Animal organic fertilizers are classified into two main types: dry and liquid. Dry animal organic fertilizers are in solid form, typically as granules or powder, and are applied to the soil by spreading or mixing. They have a longer shelf life, are easier to transport and store, and provide slow-release nutrients over a longer period of time. On the other hand, liquid animal organic fertilizers are in liquid form, usually obtained through the fermentation or extraction process, and are applied directly to the soil or through foliar spray. They are faster to absorb, more convenient for use, and ensure quicker nutrient availability to plants.</p></p>
