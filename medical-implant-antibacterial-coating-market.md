<p><p>The medical implant antibacterial coating market can be categorized into metallic and non-metallic coatings. Metallic coatings refer to antibacterial coatings that are made from metal materials such as silver, copper, or titanium. These coatings release metal ions that have antimicrobial properties, preventing the growth of bacteria on the implant's surface. On the other hand, non-metallic coatings are made from non-metal materials such as polymers or ceramics. These coatings can incorporate biocidal agents or functionalize the surface to inhibit bacterial attachment and colonization on the implant. Both types of coatings aim to reduce the risk of infection and improve the overall performance of medical implants.</p></p>
