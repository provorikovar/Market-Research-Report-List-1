<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The automotive carbon fiber composites market is divided into different types of manufacturing processes. Hand layup involves manually placing carbon fiber sheets onto a mold and applying resin by hand. Resin transfer molding uses a closed mold and injects resin under pressure. Vacuum infusion processing involves placing dry carbon fiber into a mold and applying resin under a vacuum. Injection molding uses melted resin injected into a mold to form the desired shape. Compression molding involves applying heat and pressure to shape the carbon fiber composite material into the desired form.</p></p>
