<p><ul><li>TVs Diodes</li><li>Metal Oxide Varistors</li><li>Polymeric Esd Suppressors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>ESD suppression components refer to electronic devices used to protect sensitive electronic systems from electrostatic discharges (ESDs). There are three common types of ESD suppression components: TVS diodes, metal oxide varistors (MOVs), and polymeric ESD suppressors. TVS diodes are semiconductor devices that conduct excess voltage away from the protected circuit. MOVs are voltage-dependent resistors that absorb and dissipate high levels of energy. Polymeric ESD suppressors use conductive polymers to absorb and dissipate ESD energy. All of these components serve the purpose of preventing damage to electronic systems caused by ESD.</p></p>
