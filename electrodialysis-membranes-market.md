<p><p>Electrodialysis membranes are used in electrodialysis processes, which involve the separation of ions from solutions. Heterogeneous membranes are composed of different materials and have varying pore sizes, allowing for enhanced selectivity and efficiency. Homogeneous membranes are made of a single material and offer good stability and uniformity. Bipolar membranes consist of both cationic and anionic layers, enabling the splitting of water molecules into hydrogen and hydroxide ions. These different types of membranes cater to specific needs in various industries, such as water treatment, food processing, and chemical production.</p></p>
