<p><strong>The Industrial Robots Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Articulated Robots</li><li>Parallel Robots</li><li>SCARA Robots</li><li>Cylindrical Robots</li><li>Cartesian Robots</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Industrial robots are classified into various types based on their structure and design. Articulated robots feature multiple joints that allow for maximum flexibility and movement, making them ideal for complex tasks. Parallel robots have multiple arms connected to a common base, providing high dexterity and precision for assembly operations. SCARA robots (Selective Compliance Assembly Robot Arm) are commonly used in pick-and-place operations due to their horizontal reach and vertical movement. Cylindrical robots are designed with a single rotating joint, enabling a smooth and continuous motion for handling materials. Cartesian robots have three linear axes, allowing for precise and repetitive movements in a rectangular coordinate system.</p></p>
