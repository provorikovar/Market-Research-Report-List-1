<p><p>Network slicing refers to the division of telecommunications networks into virtual segments that can be customized to suit specific requirements. SDN (Software-Defined Networking) and NFV (Network Functions Virtualization) are related market types that play a significant role in network slicing. SDN enables dynamic network configuration and management, separating control and data planes, while NFV virtualizes network functions like firewalls and load balancers. C-RAN (Cloud Radio Access Network), on the other hand, centralizes baseband processing in cloud data centers, leading to enhanced efficiency and flexibility in wireless networks.</p></p>
