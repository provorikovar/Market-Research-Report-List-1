<p><p>Refractory mortar is a specialized type of mortar used in high-temperature applications to bind bricks or other refractory materials together. There are several types of refractory mortar available in the market. Clay refractory mortar is made from clay and is suitable for low-temperature applications. High alumina refractory mortar contains high amounts of alumina and is used in high-temperature applications. Silicon refractory mortar is composed of silicon and is ideal for applications involving contact with molten metals. Magnesia refractory mortar is made from magnesia and is resistant to high-temperature and chemical erosion.</p></p>

<p><p>Refractory mortar finds widespread application in various industries including metallurgy, furnaces, kilns, and others. In metallurgy, refractory mortar is used for lining crucibles, tundishes, and ladles to withstand high temperatures and chemical erosion during the smelting process. In furnaces, refractory mortar helps to maintain the structural integrity and thermal insulation of the lining. Similarly, in kilns, it helps to sustain high temperatures without compromising the kiln's structure. In other applications, refractory mortar is used for lining chimneys, fireplaces, and other high-temperature environments to ensure heat resistance and durability.</p></p>
