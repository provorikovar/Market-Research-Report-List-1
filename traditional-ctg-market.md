<p><p>Traditional CTG (Cardiotocography) refers to a method of monitoring the fetal heart rate and uterine contractions during pregnancy. External CTG involves placing sensors on the mother's abdomen, providing non-invasive monitoring. Internal CTG, on the other hand, involves inserting an electrode into the baby's scalp, offering more accurate readings but with invasive measures. Both market types serve the purpose of assessing the well-being of the fetus and are widely used in obstetric care to detect any abnormalities or distress during labor.</p></p>
