<p><p>Microbial biosurfactants are natural compounds produced by microorganisms. Rhamnolipids and sophorolipids are two major types of biosurfactants. Rhamnolipids are mainly produced by Pseudomonas aeruginosa and have excellent surface-active and emulsifying properties. Sophorolipids, on the other hand, are produced by various yeasts, particularly Candida bombicola, and possess high foaming and emulsifying abilities. Other types of microbial biosurfactants include lipopeptides, glycolipids, and fatty acids. These biosurfactants find applications in various industries like petroleum, agriculture, food, and pharmaceuticals due to their biodegradability, low toxicity, and eco-friendly nature.</p></p>
