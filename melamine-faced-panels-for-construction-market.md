<p><p>Melamine-faced panels (MF) are commonly used in the construction industry and come in two main market types: MF-C and MF-MDF. MF-C refers to melamine-faced chipboard, which is made by bonding melamine impregnated paper to particleboard. This type of panel is durable, cost-effective, and commonly used for furniture or interior fixtures. On the other hand, MF-MDF stands for melamine-faced medium-density fiberboard. It is produced by fusing melamine resin and paper to MDF, resulting in a smoother and more consistent surface. MF-MDF is often used for high-quality furniture, cabinetry, and decorative applications.</p></p>
