<p><p>The Distributed Acoustic Sensing (DAS) System market consists of two types: Longitudinal Wave Acoustic Sensing and Transverse Wave Acoustic Sensing. Longitudinal Wave Acoustic Sensing involves the measurement of acoustic waves parallel to the direction of wave propagation. It is typically used to detect and monitor events such as earthquakes and seismic activities. On the other hand, Transverse Wave Acoustic Sensing involves the measurement of acoustic waves perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation. This type of sensing is commonly used in applications like pipeline leakage detection and perimeter security monitoring.</p></p>
