<p><p>The InP wafer market consists of two types: LEC Grown InP and VGF Grown InP. LEC Grown InP refers to Indium Phosphide wafers grown using the Liquid Encapsulated Czochralski method. This method produces high-quality wafers suitable for various applications such as fiber optics and photonic devices. On the other hand, VGF Grown InP refers to wafers grown using the Vertical Gradient Freeze method. These wafers are known for their lower cost and are commonly used in microwave and millimeter-wave devices. Both types cater to different market segments based on their specific characteristics and applications.</p></p>
