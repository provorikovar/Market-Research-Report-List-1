<p><p>The rare hematology treatment market includes two main types: plasma derived factors and recombinant factors. Plasma derived factors involve using specific proteins extracted from human plasma to treat rare blood disorders. These proteins, like clotting factors, can be deficient or dysfunctional in patients. Recombinant factors, on the other hand, are synthetic versions of these proteins that are produced through genetic engineering techniques. Both plasma derived factors and recombinant factors play a crucial role in treating rare hematological conditions and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
