<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>In the flexible printing market, there are three main types of inks commonly used: water-based inks, solvent-based inks, and UV-cured inks.</p><p>Water-based inks are made by suspending pigments in water. They are non-toxic, environmentally friendly, and have low volatile organic compound (VOC) content. However, they may require longer drying times and show limited resistance to water and abrasion.</p><p>Solvent-based inks are made by dissolving pigments in organic solvents. They provide fast drying times, excellent adhesion, and resistance to water and abrasion. However, they contain high VOC levels, making them less eco-friendly.</p><p>UV-cured inks are formulated to dry instantly upon exposure to ultraviolet light. They offer high color brilliance, superior adhesion, and excellent resistance against water and abrasion. However, they require special curing equipment and may have higher costs.</p></p>
