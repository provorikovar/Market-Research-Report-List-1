<p><p>The aircraft fuel containment market includes two types: flexible fuel containment and rigid fuel containment. Flexible fuel containment refers to fuel tanks made from flexible materials such as rubber or composite materials, which can expand or contract depending on the amount of fuel stored. Rigid fuel containment, on the other hand, involves fuel tanks made from rigid materials such as metal, which have a fixed shape and cannot expand or contract. Both types of fuel containment ensure the safe storage and transportation of fuel in aircraft.</p></p>
